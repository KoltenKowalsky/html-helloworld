About Me

Kolten is a thought leader in implementing enterprise technologies on $100 MM dollar opportunity values. Kolten's background is a hybrid of business and technical skills needed in the Digital Information Age. He has completed over 300 end-to-end net new solution deployments, small-to-massive efforts with 10-100 project team sizes. He is an expert in Change Management from incremental to transformational, budgets ranging $1 to $20 MM on multi-month/year initiatives. He helps develop business cases and best fit technical solution options using vendor platform capabilities and internal/external resources.

His 20+ years career leading digital lifecycles provides tremendous insights into high performance results and managing risks. Kolten has a MBA in Management of Technology from Georgia Tech, certified PMP for over 10 years and takes continuous training courses. Kolten resides in Atlanta, Georgia USA. He is originally from Saint John, Indiana.

Each type of dataset could be used individually or collectively to best provide “storytelling” for certain pieces, parts, or the entire program solution, such as a playbook or collection of artifacts in compliance with TOGAF, SAFe, PMO and other governance guidelines.

Kolten uses artifacts, as storytelling techniques, to run successful meetings, team build, solution develop and justify options. His super strength is to arrange unstructured data into dataset functions, showing direct and indirect dependencies across business processes and IT system stacks.

Walk-me-through plans and keeping discussions on point can be better accomplished!

As a result, audiences (stakeholders, leads and functional resources) gain faster orientation, clarification, and next steps direction.
